Charged Particles

The magnitude of an electric field at a point due to a single particle can be calculated using the formula E = kq/r2

q is the charge of the particle, r is the distance from the particle, and k is the proportionality constant, also known as the Coulomb constant, which is approximately 9 x 109 N m2/C2

When the particle has a positive charge, this field points directly away from the particle, and vice versa for a negatively charged particle

When there is multiple particles, taking the x, y, and z components by using trigonometry tells us the direction and magnitude of the electric field at any point
